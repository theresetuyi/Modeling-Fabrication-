# Day 5 Digital Fabrication I: Laser Cutting
Overview
On Day 5, the focus was on laser cutting technology and its application in designing and fabricating a mechanical mounting bracket. The objective of the session was to understand how 2D digital design can be translated into a precise physical component using a laser cutting machine.
The activity emphasized preparing accurate designs, understanding machine operation, and following proper safety and fabrication procedures.
# Design Preparation for Laser Cutting
The research covered the essential principles of laser cutting design, including:
•	Creating accurate 2D vector drawings suitable for laser cutting
•	Ensuring all dimensions were correctly defined for functional assembly
•	Designing clean, closed paths to enable smooth and continuous cutting
Special attention was given to designing components that are easy to modify and adaptable to different materials or thicknesses.
# Laser Cutting Process
The laser cutting workflow involved:
•	Selecting appropriate materials such as acrylic, plywood, or thin metal sheets
•	Setting correct laser parameters, including power, speed, and frequency
•	Properly positioning and securing the material on the laser cutter bed
•	Executing test cuts to confirm accuracy before full production
These steps ensured clean cuts, minimal material waste, and consistent results.
# Mechanical Design Considerations
Good mechanical design practices were applied during the process, such as:
•	Adding fillets and rounded corners to reduce stress concentration
•	Including weight-reduction cut-outs while maintaining structural strength
•	Ensuring correct hole placement and alignment for mounting and fastening
•	Designing with assembly and usability in mind
The resulting mounting bracket was functional, lightweight, and suitable for real-world use.
# Safety and Best Practices
Safety was a key focus throughout the session. Proper procedures included:
•	Wearing appropriate safety equipment
•	Ensuring adequate ventilation and exhaust operation
•	Never leaving the laser cutter unattended during operation
•	Following machine-specific safety guidelines
