# Activity 5.Digital Fabrication I: Laser Cutting
## Introduction

Laser cutting is a digital fabrication process that uses a focused, high-power
laser beam to cut or engrave materials such as wood, acrylic, plastics, and
thin metals. Its key advantages are high precision, speed, and repeatability,
making it suitable for both prototyping and production.

The laser cutter works through an integrated system. Mirrors and a focusing
lens guide and concentrate the laser beam, while the motion system moves the
laser accurately along X and Y axes. The workbed supports the material, and
exhaust and cooling systems manage heat, smoke, and fumes to protect both
the machine and the operator.

## After Class Activity:Laser Cutting a Design

On this day we learned how to operate a laser cutting machine. We accessed
the machine through its IP address on a computer connected to the same
network. This allowed us to control the machine digitally without a physical
connection.

## Step 1 Accessing the Machine

We connected to the laser cutter by entering its IP address into a browser
on a computer connected to the same network. The machine control interface
appeared, showing options for power, speed, and cutting paths.

**Laser cutter control panel:**

## Step 2 Preparing the Design

We drew our design directly in the machine's software interface. The design
included a world map image to be engraved onto the material. We controlled:

- The position of the laser head on the material
- The number of passes to control engraving depth
- The speed of the laser movement
- The power level for engraving vs cutting

## Step 3  Running the Machine

Once the design was ready and the material was positioned on the workbed,
we started the job. The machine first ran the engraving passes to burn the
world map image into the surface of the material. We monitored the process
throughout to ensure it was running correctly and safely.

## Step 4 Final Result

After the laser completed all passes, we removed the material from the
workbed. The world map was cleanly engraved onto the surface of the black
material, showing good detail and consistent depth across the design.

## Safety Practices

Throughout the activity we followed proper safety procedures:

- Never left the machine unattended while running
- Ensured the exhaust system was active to remove fumes
- Kept hands away from the machine during operation
- Used the E-STOP button visible on the control panel for emergencies
## What I Learned

This activity showed me how digital design connects directly to physical
fabrication. A design drawn on screen is translated into precise machine
movements that produce a real physical object. Controlling parameters like
speed, power, and number of passes gave me a practical understanding of
how laser cutting settings affect the final result.
